Why WynneWorks

  • We give landowners the ability to install solar and reap the benefits with no upfront cost.
  • We develop small-scale solar projects in Oregon and Washington—typically 1 MW systems on about 5 acres or on large commercial rooftops over 50,000 sq ft.
  • Our projects deliver both financial and environmental benefits to you and your community.
  • We handle development, interconnection, financing, construction, and long-term operations, while landowners earn stable lease income and keep their land.

We Believe

  • In giving landowners the power to preserve land for future generations.
  • In educating, counseling, and guiding landowners through each step so the small solar project fits your goals and your land.
  • In the benefits of small renewable projects — sponsored by landowners like you — that strengthen local grids, improve local tax bases, and help families build long-term wealth.
  • We create Wynnefalls!™

Heather Eberhardt

Heather Eberhardt has built a diverse background that spans multiple fields. Over her 17-year career, the principal of our company has developed deep expertise in solar energy development, encompassing every stage of the process—from site evaluation and product design to permitting, pricing, contracting, and financing.

Experience Highlights Include

  • Project finance, procurement, and contracting for one of the world’s largest renewable energy companies on projects in Oregon, New Jersey, Canada, Kansas, California, and Texas.
  • Project development—including site due diligence, interconnection, asset acquisition and sales, power purchase agreements, and permitting—for another major renewable energy company on solar projects in California, Colorado, and Minnesota.
  • Energy procurement and contracting for a large Pacific Northwest public utility.
  • Project management and development—including site evaluation, land leasing and easement negotiations, permitting, interconnection, budgeting, and logistics—for a major energy holding company on solar projects in California and Texas.
  • Over 1,000 megawatts (1,000,000 kilowatts, or 1 billion watts) of installed solar capacity.
  • Heather has worked for some of the largest and most influential renewable energy companies in the United States. Now she wants to bring that expertise home where it matters most.
